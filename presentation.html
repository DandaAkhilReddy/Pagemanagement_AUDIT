Page Management RCM Assessment
November 2025

Volume Context

  • Daily Claims: 800-900 transmitted to clearinghouse
  • Daily Posting Target: $100,000+
  • Staff: 17 employees (Page Management + HHA billing staff)
  • Geographic Coverage: Florida (Primary), Texas (Secondary)

Technology Stack

  • EMR: Meditech → IngeniousMed (auto-capture) → AdvancedMD (PM system)
  • Clearinghouse: McKesson (7 PM auto-transmit + manual pushes)
  • Payment Posting: 100% manual, no automation configured
  • Reporting: Excel-based, SFTP file drops

Critical Limitation

Single Point of Failure: Heavy reliance on Lisa (Page Management owner) for critical operations. When volume increases, work postpones to tomorrow - creating rolling backlogs.

Priority Issue Impact
CRITICAL Physician Coding & Documentation Most physicians lack training, skip Level 3 codes to avoid documentation - lost revenue per encounter
CRITICAL Physician Billing Delay 15 days given vs 72 hours needed. Lisa starts billing after 12-day delay - revenue cycle elongation
CRITICAL Payment Posting Backlog 100% manual, no automation. Cannot scale with volume - Lisa postpones work when overloaded
HIGH Payment Tracking Gaps Most insurances don't pay on EOB dates. No follow-up system, no automation to track delays
HIGH Bank Reconciliation Gap Weekly (not daily), manual matching - unexplained discrepancies, audit risk

Current State

  • Most physicians do not know exact codes to use
  • No proper training provided to physicians
  • Multiple doctors interviewed confirm incorrect coding
  • Exception: Dr. Veena Reddy is ONLY physician coding correctly

Root Cause

  • Level 3 CPT codes require more documentation
  • Doctors skip to lower-level codes to avoid documentation effort
  • Prioritize patient care over billing accuracy
  • No feedback loop showing revenue impact

Financial Impact

  • Undercoding = lost revenue on every encounter
  • Cannot quantify total revenue loss without comprehensive coding audit
  • Compliance risk if audited by payers (unbundling/upcoding perception if corrected later)
  • Perpetuates incorrect behavior without intervention

Action Owner

Monthly coding audits comparing documentation to codes billed External coding auditor
Use Dr. Veena Reddy's coding as internal benchmark Coding Educator

Current: 15 Days Given

  • Doctors have 15 days to complete billing
  • Many doctors don't bill same day
  • After 15 days: Lisa's team starts billing for unbilled encounters
  • 12-day delay between encounter and billing start
  • Incomplete documentation remains an issue
  • Missing modifiers (especially Q6 for locum providers)

Should Be: 72 Hours Maximum

  • Strict 72-hour window for physician billing
  • After 72 hours, billing team takes over immediately
  • Reduces revenue cycle by 12 days
  • Aligns with industry best practice
  • Enables faster denial identification

Impact

  • 12-day revenue delay on every unbilled encounter
  • Cash flow impact compounds across 800-900 daily claims
  • Delayed denial identification (can't appeal until claim submitted)
  • AR aging artificially inflated by unbilled encounters

Humana Special Issue (October 2025 Change)

30-Day Pending Letter Scheme: After medical records uploaded, Humana auto-generates pending letter adding 30+ days to payment timeline. Requires systematic tracking of request dates, upload dates, invoices ($2/page per Florida statute), and interest calculations for late payments beyond 30 days.

Required Solution

  • Automated tracking: Expected deposit date vs actual deposit date
  • Daily alerts when lag exceeds payer-specific threshold
  • Escalation workflow for missing payments (auto-generate follow-up tasks)
  • Humana pending letter tracking dashboard

Current State

  • Weekly reconciliation (not daily)
  • 100% manual matching process
  • Unexplained discrepancies exist
  • Bank labels don't match payer names (e.g., "Optum" vs "United Healthcare")
  • EOBs not always received for all deposits
  • Deposits sometimes split across multiple days

Should Be: Daily Three-Way Match

  • Daily reconciliation (not weekly)
  • Three-way matching:
    • EOB received
    • Payment posted in system
    • Actual bank deposit
  • Same-day discrepancy identification
  • Automated payer-to-bank name mapping

Impact

  • Incomplete/delayed matching between claims submitted, remittances received, payments posted, and bank deposits
  • Audit risk (cannot quickly prove all deposits matched to claims)
  • Obscures true cash position (finance doesn't know what's unposted)
  • Cannot quickly identify missing payments or payer underpayments
  • Weekly process cannot keep pace with 800-900 daily claims

Two Paths Forward

Path A: Automation Investment

  • Deploy RPA for payment posting (75% automation rate)
  • Implement automated tracking dashboards
  • Configure auto-posting rules in AdvancedMD
  • Build payer lag monitoring system
  • Outcome: Scale with existing team
  • ROI: 3-month payback

Path B: In-House Transition

  • Bring billing operations in-house
  • Hire dedicated billing manager
  • Build internal team with cross-training
  • Still requires automation investment (same tools needed)
  • Outcome: Direct control + scalability
  • Investment: Higher upfront, ongoing payroll
